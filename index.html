About

Kohei YAMAMOTO, a Japanese software engineer and researcher, has lived and worked in Japan and Singapore. He first ventured into computer science by programming CPU architecture during his undergraduate studies. At 21, he began to delve into locationally-focused micro studies, specifically wireless localization, while collaborating with one of the internet pioneers.

Graduating at the top of his undergraduate class, he then took a one-year hiatus to explore different layers of the IT industry, gaining practical experience from a prestigious research institution, leading tech companies, a Japanese unicorn, and an American startup. During this time, he was actively involved in various data science projects.

Following his gap year, he pursued a master's degree, focusing more on locationally-macro studies and solidifying his research interests in spatiotemporal analysis, human mobility, and computational social science. After graduating with distinction, he served as a full-time research staff at the university while honing his analytical skills at a Japanese startup.

Currently, he is an Azure App Innovation Specialist, helping businesses transition to the cloud and envisioning their future. Simultaneously, he continues his work as a spatiotemporal researcher at the university, demonstrating his skills in data modeling & integrations considering environmental-behavioural parameters.
